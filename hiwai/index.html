Understanding Hydrogen's Impact on Climate

HIWAI is a pioneering research project investigating how hydrogen emissions affect atmospheric chemistry and contribute to climate change. Our work bridges science and policy to shape a sustainable hydrogen economy.

Why Hydrogen Matters for Climate

As the world transitions toward hydrogen as a clean energy carrier, understanding the environmental implications of hydrogen emissions becomes critical.

While hydrogen itself is not a direct greenhouse gas, it plays an important role in atmospheric chemistry. When released into the atmosphere, hydrogen can affect the lifetime of methane and influence stratospheric water vapor levels.

HIWAI provides the scientific foundation needed for informed decision-making in the emerging hydrogen economy.
