April 5, 2024

Partnership Announced with Leading Climate Research Institutions

HIWAI is expanding its collaborative network with new partnerships with three leading climate research institutions, enhancing our capacity for global-scale hydrogen emissions monitoring. These partnerships will enable us to deploy measurement stations across multiple continents and share data in real-time.

Partnership Collaboration
March 10, 2024

HIWAI Team Presents at International Climate Conference

Members of the HIWAI research team presented their findings on hydrogen emissions and climate feedback mechanisms at the International Conference on Climate Science in Vienna. The presentation highlighted preliminary results from our atmospheric modeling work, showing how hydrogen emissions could influence global methane concentrations over the coming decades.

Conference Presentation
February 20, 2024

New Study Reveals Hydrogen's Role in Atmospheric Chemistry

Our latest research published in Nature Climate Change demonstrates that hydrogen emissions can significantly influence methane lifetime in the atmosphere, with important implications for climate mitigation strategies. The study used a combination of observational data and modeling to quantify hydrogen's indirect warming potential.

Publication Atmospheric Chemistry
January 15, 2024

HIWAI Project Officially Launches

We are thrilled to announce the official launch of the HIWAI (Hydrogen Impacts on Water and Atmospheric Interactions) research project. Our interdisciplinary team will investigate the climate implications of hydrogen emissions as the world transitions to a hydrogen economy. The project brings together researchers from four leading institutions across five European countries.
