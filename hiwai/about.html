About HIWAI

Understanding hydrogen's role in climate change through collaborative research

Our Mission

As the world transitions toward hydrogen as a clean energy carrier, understanding the environmental implications of hydrogen emissions becomes critical. HIWAI aims to provide the scientific foundation needed for informed decision-making in the emerging hydrogen economy.

Our mission is to quantify hydrogen emissions, understand their atmospheric chemistry, and assess their impact on climate change. We work to bridge the gap between scientific research and policy implementation.

🌎

Why Hydrogen Matters

Understanding the climate implications of the hydrogen economy

H2

While hydrogen itself is not a direct greenhouse gas, it plays an important role in atmospheric chemistry. When released into the atmosphere, hydrogen can affect the lifetime of methane (a potent greenhouse gas) and influence stratospheric water vapor levels.

As hydrogen production and use expand globally, understanding these indirect climate effects is essential for ensuring that the hydrogen transition delivers its promised climate benefits.

HIWAI's research addresses critical questions about hydrogen leakage rates, atmospheric chemistry, and climate feedbacks that will shape the future of clean energy.

Our Approach

Combining field research, laboratory experiments, and advanced modeling

🔍

Field Measurements

Deploying advanced instrumentation to measure hydrogen concentrations and emissions at key locations around the world.

🔬

Laboratory Studies

Conducting controlled experiments to understand hydrogen's chemical reactions in the atmosphere.

💻

Atmospheric Modeling

Using state-of-the-art climate models to simulate hydrogen cycling and predict future scenarios.

📈

Data Analysis

Applying advanced statistical methods and machine learning to extract insights from complex datasets.

Research Objectives

Our key goals for advancing hydrogen-climate science

🎯 Quantify Emissions

Measure hydrogen emissions from natural sources (soils, oceans) and anthropogenic sources (industry, transport, energy) to establish comprehensive emission inventories.

🔬 Model Chemistry

Develop and refine atmospheric chemistry models to accurately represent hydrogen's interactions with other atmospheric constituents and climate feedbacks.

📡 Develop Monitoring

Create standardized protocols and tools for monitoring hydrogen emissions, enabling industry and regulators to track and minimize leakage.

📝 Guide Policy

Translate scientific findings into clear recommendations for policymakers designing hydrogen strategies and regulations.

🎓 Train Researchers

Build capacity by training the next generation of scientists in hydrogen-climate research methods and interdisciplinary approaches.

🤝 Foster Collaboration

Build international partnerships with research institutions, industry, and policymakers to maximize research impact.
